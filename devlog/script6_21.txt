Where am I as of 6/21:

I've spent a lot of time writing a lot of the same code over and over

Now, you make look at me and think I'm crazy, why would this man spend time writing his own engine when there are plenty of premade engines out there
Especially since you're solo dev??

And there's a whole lot of reasons in which I believe spending time to make your own engine is incredibly important
    - Maybe an engine is making decisions about the way it works that don't fit your wants and needs
    - Maybe you believe the engine that already exists is needlessly complicated for what you want to make
    - Maybe the engines out there are poorly constructed and force you to work in a way that limits your productivty and therefore ability to be creative and then you end up making the same game that everyone else is making when you want to make experiences that no one has experienced before and in order to do that you spend 3 years learning C, learning graphics programming, learning 3D math and after 3 years only have a cube on the screen to show for your progress

    - Or maybe you just enjoy programming

So, to combat the repeated code issue I started to develop my own APIs
It's through these APIs that I then simply call the functions that were being repeated to have a faster start up time when it came to a new idea


When it comes to engine architecture, while I am definitely not the pro when it comes to this, a lot of what I have heard is that you want to avoid Immediate Mode Rendering

This essentially means that we don't want our game code (cameras, entities, everthing that the game requires) to be intertwined with our platform layer (think the ways that Dx11 creates and fills buffers vs whatever OpenGL does)
This way all we have to do is take our game layer and the functions that are to be called in it, and simply plop it on top of whatever platform we decide to use, plug and play type of situation

What this entails however is far more difficult, especially for 3D games (this is where it gets fun)

So given this first obstacle, one of the major things we need to avoid is wrapping our game code around the platform
This means:
 - Our camera shouldn't be based on our platform
 - Input response shouldn't be based on our platform
 - Loading things shouldn't be based on our platform

What does this mean physically? Well it means that I took the time to develop a somewhat custom math library so that our camera doesn't have to exist in DirectX land
(All I basically did was take a look at the backend of DirectXMath and assimilate using my own types for vectors and matrices)
